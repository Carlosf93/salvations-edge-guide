INTRODUCTION

Recurring buffs and debuffs

Final Shape Looming/Imminent End/The Final Shape

Countdown timer debuff present at the top of the screen in every encounter.

  • Final Shape Looming: Substratum, Dissipation and Repository
  • Imminent End: Verity
  • The Final Shape: Zenith
  • If the countdown ends, it will wipe the entire fireteam
  • The max timer is different for every encounter

Resonance

Resonance is the main mechanic buff for the first three encounters of the raid.

  • Stackable up to x3. Picking up a 4th stack will kill you
  • 1 minute 30 seconds debuff timer. If the timer ends you die
  • Picking up a stack will reset the debuff timer
  • Using one resonance to close a resonant conductor will reset the debuff timer
  • Each stack dunked at a resonant altar will increase the Final Shape Looming timer by 20 seconds
  • There are three different types of resonance: Pyramidal, Spherical and Hexahedron
  • Picking up a resonance will overwrite your entire stack to the last picked up resonance type

Types of Resonance

Pyramidal Resonance Referred to as Triangle
Spherical Resonance Referred to as Circle
Hexahedron Resonance Referred to as Square
  • A variant of the resonance buff is featured in the last encounter.

Recurring Mechanic Elements

Resonant Altar

Interactable chest linked to the resonance mechanic.

  • You can only interact with it if you have resonance stacks
  • Used to dunk your resonance to increase the Final Shape Looming debuff timer
  • It will only spawn after all resonant conductors are closed and despawn once every member of the fireteam dunks their resonance in it
  • The first guardian dunk will lock out the rest of the fireteam from dunking resonance for a few seconds. Unclear if it is a bug or intended behaviour
  • Sometimes dunking will not get rid of all your stacks. Make sure the resonance debuff is gone before assuming you are done with your resonance dunking

Resonant Plates

Resonant plates are the co-op mechanic used to generate resonance on the first three encounters of the raid.

  • To generate resonance we will always use two resonant plates: a Starting plate and a Closing plate. Both assigned to one fireteam member each
  • The Starting plate and the Closing plate are connected by a dark orange wire
  • A pair of plates will be deactivated by default at the start of every encounter. The condition to activate them will depend on the encounter
  • When a pair of plates is activated the Starting plate will have an orange glow

Plates Mechanics

Stepping on an activated Starting plate will send the orange glow via the wire to the Closing plate.

When the glow reaches the Closing plate a shrinking orange glow circle will appear. Stepping on the plate before the circle completely shrinks will start a Plate Chain and send the glow back to the Starting Plate, which will now act the same as the Closing Plate when the glow reaches it.

For every correctly (before the circle closes) stepped on plate a Set of Resonance will spawn. The amount and type of resonance in a Set of Resonance are different for every encounter. Three Sets of Resonance is the max amount of resonance needed for an encounter.

Stepping too early (before the glow reaches the plate) or too late (after the circle completely shrinks) onto a plate will break the Plate Chain.

The consequence of breaking a Plate Chain is different for every encounter.

Resonant Conductor

The resonant conductor is the end goal of the plates mechanics.

  • It is always next to the Closing Plate
  • The goal of the member of the fireteam assigned to the Closing Plate is to close the conductor
  • A conductor only closes with one specific resonance type. Using the wrong resonance type to close a conductor will wipe the entire fireteam
  • To close a conductor a guardian needs to shoot the glowing resonance inside the conductor while having at least x1 resonance stack. If done correctly the conductor will show damage numbers. The next time the Closing Plate is correctly stepped on (shrinking circle) the conductor will close and the guardian will lose a resonance stack
  • Closing a Resonant Conductor will despawn every resonance spawned by its resonant plates that has not been picked up

    • States

    • Deactivated: The pair of plates it is linked to are deactivated
    • Activated: The pair of plates is activated. The conductor is open and it shows the resonance type it needs to close.
    • Ready to close: Conductor has been shot and its resonance glows a bit brighter than their ‘Activated’ state.
    • Closed: The entire conductor has a bright, light orange glow. The plates it is linked to deactivate.

Example: Resonance Generation and Conductor Closing

  • When a pair of plates are activated and the Starting Plate is glowing the Starter Guardian will step on their plate
  • When the glow reaches the Closing Plate the Closer Guardian will step on the plate while the glowing circle is shrinking, starting a Plate Chain and spawning the first Set of Resonance. The glow will bounce back to the Starting Plate
  • When the glow reaches the Starting Plate the Starter Guardian will step on the plate while the glowing circle is shrinking, spawning the second Set of Resonance. The glow will bounce back to the Closing Plate
  • When the glow reaches the Closing Plate the Closer Guardian will step onto the plate while the glowing circle is shrinking, spawning the last Set of Resonance. The glow will bounce back to the Starting Plate
  • Ideally both Guardians would pick up the spawned resonance between plate bounces and keep the Plate Chain alive until all resonance has been picked up even if no more Resonance Sets will spawn
  • When both guardians have picked up all their corresponding spawned resonance, the Closer Guardian will shoot the Resonant Conductor, setting it to its ‘Ready to Close’ state. When the glow bounces back to the Closing Plate the Closer Guardian will correctly step on it, closing the Resonant Conductor

TRAVERSAL I

Bonus Rooms

Shortly after the first platforming section you will enter the monolith and get to a room with a set of stairs on each side that lead to a wall that has eight columns of five resonance symbols each.

Every column is the same if you restart the raid except for the third one. The third column is the one you want to pay attention to and take notes of its symbols FROM BOTTOM TO TOP. Each of this symbols will be the resonance used to close the conductor in its corresponding bonus room.
Using the wrong resonance to close any of the conductors will lock every subsequent room, preventing you from getting the rewards.

Room Rewards

  • Deepsight weapon chest (1/3)
  • Raid mod after defeating The Witness (LOCKED)
  • Deepsight weapon chest (2/3)
  • Lore entry after defeating The Witness (LOCKED)
  • Deepsight weapon chest (3/3)

Every secret room has a pair of active plates and a conductor. Generate resonance using the plates and close the conductor with the corresponding resonance to complete the room.

SUBSTRATUM

Enemies
Loot
End Goal

Spawn the resonant chest and dunk enough resonance into it to survive the Final Shape Looming three times.

Final Shape Looming

4 minutes 15 seconds

Set of Resonance

Three Triangle resonances:

One in the Starting Plate room for the Starter Guardian

One in the Closing Plate room for the Closer Guardian

One in the large central room for the Ad-Clearer Guardian

Plate Chain Break

Mini-Boss Subjugator will spawn in the Starting Plate room, locking the Plates until it is dead

Enviroment

The encounter takes place in a total of nine rooms: a large central room and eight smaller rooms.

The large central room has a resonant chest at the beginning of the encounter and a door on each wall and in each corner that leads to one of the smaller rooms.

Each of the smaller rooms has one resonant plate and one resonant conductor. The resonant plate has two wires that connect it to the plates in the adjacent smaller rooms.

Roles

Wall Team
  • Starter
  • Closer
  • Ad-Clearer
Corner Team
  • Starter
  • Closer
  • Ad-Clearer

Encounter Explanation

Witness Arm: Triangle

The arm will target one guardian and spawn a glowing triangular attack that explodes after a few seconds, damaging every player inside it.

When every fireteam member is in the center room all the doors leading to the smaller rooms will close. If someone picks up the triangle resonance and dunks it in the resonant chest, the chest will despawn, a triangle witness arm will spawn, and the encounter starts.

Two of the doors will open, one in a wall and the other in a corner of that same wall. Vex suplicants will come out of the rooms those doors lead to. The Wall Team will enter the the room that the wall door leads to, and the Corner team will enter the room the corner door leads to. These two doors will close.

The teams will be moving in opposite directions clearing all the ads inside their rooms and helping the other team progress. When one of the teams (Team A) kills a vex minotaur in their room a vex hydra will spawn in the room the other team (Team B) is in. When Team B kills that hydra the door that Team A needs to progress through will open.



Eventually one of the teams will have an Overload Champion spawn in one of their rooms, the earliest a team can find the Overload Champion is the third room. If that team is too slow to kill it, the other team will have their Overload Champion spawn in their fourth room.

When the Overload Champion is dead all the doors that lead to the center room will open and a Boss Tormentor will spawn in it. Killing the Tormentor will activate the plate in the room the Overload Champion was killed, this is the first of two Starting Plates we need to complete one cycle of the encounter. The Closing Plate that pairs with that Starting Plate will be in one of the rooms connected to it by wires, next to an active Resonant Conductor.

Killing the Tormentor will also close all the doors again and a new set of two doors (Wall and Corner) will open. Both teams will repeat the same process to progress through the rooms, killing minotaurs, hydras and eventually the Overload Champion. The team that does not spawn the Overload Champion first (the one that would spawn it in their fourth room instead of their third) will most likely come across the first active Starting Plate or the Resonant Conductor next to the Closing Plate it leads to; this will be that team's pair of plates. After the other team kills the Overload Champion a new Tormentor will spawn. Killing it will activate the plate in the room where the second Overload Champion died, this will be the second set of Starting Plate and Closing Plate/Conductor needed to complete the cycle. The Team that killed the Overload Champion in that room will be in charge of dealing with this set of plates.



After the second Tormentor is dead and each team knows where their set of Starting and Closing Plates is, it is time to start the Resonace generation with the plates. The Starter Guardian in each team will step on the Starting Plate, the Closer Guardian will return the ping and start the Plate Chain. Both will keep the chain alive until all Sets of Resonance (three total) have spawned.

The Ad-Clearers are free to pick up their resonance whenever they want from the center room. The Starter and Closer Guardians will need to pick up theirs between Plate pings, being careful not to neglect the plate for too long, or they may break the Plate Chain.

When everyone on a team has picked up all their resonance (everyone should have x3 resonance), it is time to close the conductor, the Closer Guardian will shoot the triangle in the open conductor and successfully return a plate ping to close it.

When both teams (Wall and Corner) have closed their Conductor a Mini-Boss Subjugator will spawn in the center room alongside a few extra ads. Killing every enemy in the room will respawn the Resonant Chest, allowing everyone in the fireteam to dunk their resonance.

Once everyone has dunked their resonance, the first cycle of three needed to end the encounter is complete.



The other two cycles will be exactly the same.

Enviroment

Left, Mid, and Right areas with structures, a Starting Plate at the top of the structure and a Closing Plate at the bottom, right next to the conductor.

All the areas are connected to a large square arena with a taken blight in the center.

Roles

Left Area
Mid Area
Right Area

Each team will have a Starter and a Closer. You can decide with your partner if you want to assign roles or leave it to the Call to Reckoning RNG.

Encounter Explanation

Witness Arm: Circle

The arm will spawn glowing circular attacks at a fixed location, darkness projectiles will slowly approach the circle, damaging every player that is inside it when they reach it.

Stolen Valor

With the introduction of the Circle resonance in this encounter, a new buff is also introduced.

When one of the Conductors is active it will show an undefined rotating shape instead of the resonance type it needs to close. To know which shape closes the Conductor, you will need to have the Stolen Valor buff.

If you have the Stolen Valor buff, instead of the undefined rotating shape you will see either a triangle resonance shape or a circle resonance shape. Whichever you see is the one needed to close the Conductor.

You don't need Stolen Valor to close the conductor with the correct shape; it is just an informative buff.

Each pair of Guardians will go to their areas. When everyone is ready, shoot the taken blight in the center of the square arena to start the encounter.

Enemies will spawn on both sides of an area. When the first set of enemies is dead, another set will spawn on one of the sides, alongside a taken Mini-Boss. The Mini-Boss will be different for each area:

Left: Taken Wizard
Mid: Taken Phalanx
Right: Taken Ogre

While killing the Mini-Boss, one of the Guardians in the team will randomly receive the Call to Reckoning buff. After the Mini-Boss is dead, the pair of plates and the conductor will activate.

Call to Reckoning

Call to Reckoning is the pre-buff for Stolen Valor.

To transform your Call to Reckoning into Stolen Valor all three selected guardians will need to have a small fight against the boss in the square arena.

When all three Called guardians are ready, they will enter the boss arena, a weak spot on the boss's head will appear. Breaking the weak spot will spawn a small taken blight on top of the boss's head and bigger taken blights in the Square Arena and the Left, Mid, and Right areas.

When all bigger taken blights have been destroyed the smaller blight on the boss head will disappear, the weak spot will appear again and destroying it for a second time will turn all the Call to Reckoning buffs to Stolen Valor.

Depending on when you want to handle the Call to Reckoning buff, there are two strategies to complete the encounter.

Strat A: Plates Before Reckoning

When one of the guardians gets the Call to Reckoning buff, they will ignore it for now.

When the Mini-Boss is dead, one of the Guardians will go to the Starting Plate, and the other one will stay at the Closing Plate. They will spawn all three resonance sets (one Triangle and one Circle for each set, making a total of six resonance, three for each Guardian in the team) and leave the resonance where they spawn without picking it up yet. All three teams should be doing this simultaneously.

When all teams are done spawning the resonace, the Called Guardian in each team will go to the boss fight to transform their buff.

The Guardian that was not called will kill the Mini-Boss that respawns after the Plate Chain breaks.

Once the Called Guardian has Stolen Valor, they will go back to their area, take a look at the conductor, communicate to their partner which resonance type they need to close it, pick up all three of that resonance type, go to the Closing Plate and wait for the non-Called Guardian to ping from the Starting Plate so they can close.

Pros: Pinging the plates without having to worry about ads or picking up resonance in time between ping makes the encounter easier.

Cons: If one or more of the teams is too slow with the plates, it may result in a delay for the Called Guardians' boss fight, making the mechanics portion of the encounter a bit slower.

Strat B: Plates After Reckoning

As soon as the Guardian in each team gets the Call to Reckoning buff, they go to the center arena and fight the boss to transform the buff into Stolen Valor.

When they are finished with the fight, they will return to their area, take a look at the conductor, communicate which resonance they need to close the Conductor to their partner and stay at the bottom plate; they will be the Closer.

The Guardian without Stolen Valor will go to the top of the structure; they will be the Starter.

Both of them start pinging the plates to spawn the three resonances sets (one Triangle and one Circle for each set, making a total of six resonance, three for each Guardian in the team), pick up the resonance between pings and close the conductor with the correct resonance when all of it has been picked up.

Pros: This strategy is faster.

Cons: Dealing with the enemies (specially the attendants) while pinging plates and trying to pick up resonance between pings makes the encounter considerably harder.

After all three teams close their Conductor, the resonant chest will spawn in the side of the square arena not connected to any of the areas. Every Guardian should dunk their resonance to extend the timer and get ready to fight the boss.

The damage phase starts when the square arena walls glow and lasts around 30 seconds. After that, if the boss has not reached Final Stand, the wall's glow will dim and a new cycle of mechanics will begin.



On Final Stand, the damage phase time will extend, and extra enemies will spawn in the boss arena until it is dead.

REPOSITORY

Enemies
Loot
End Goal

Gather resonance to extend the timer long enough for the fireteam to survive the three rooms

Final Shape Looming

3 minutes 30 seconds

Set of Resonance

Two resonances:

One near the Starting Plate

One near the Closing Plate

Plate Chain Break

Mini-Boss Subjugator will spawn near the closing plate, locking the Plates until it is dead

Enviroment

The encounter takes place across three different rooms.

Every room is bigger than the previous one but all of them have the same layout: three starting plates near the entrance and three closing plates next to a conductor near the exit.

Starting Plates are connected to the Closing Plates via the wires, the connections between plates are more scrambled the further the room you are in.

Roles

Left
  • Starter
  • Closer
Mid
  • Starter
  • Closer
Right
  • Starter
  • Closer

Encounter Explanation

Witness Arm: Square

The arm charges and shoots a horizontal darkness beam parallel to the ground, damaging every player that is hit by it.

Resonance Spawns

The Resonance in a set spawns along the wire that connects two plates. The resonance type will depend on the Starting Plate location.

  • Left Starting Plate always spawns Square
  • Mid Starting Plate always spawns Circle
  • Right Starting Plate always spawns Triangle

First Room

  • Square: near the Left Starting Plate and the Left Closing Plate
  • Circle: near the Mid Starting Plate and the Mid Closing Plate
  • Triangle: near the Right Starting Plate and the Right Closing Plate

Second Room

  • Square: near the Left Starting Plate and the Mid Closing Plate
  • Circle: near the Mid Starting Plate and the Left Closing Plate
  • Triangle: near the Right Starting Plate and the Right Closing Plate

Third Room

  • Square: near the Left Starting Plate and the Mid Closing Plate
  • Circle: near the Mid Starting Plate and the Right Closing Plate
  • Triangle: near the Right Starting Plate and the Left Closing Plate

For this encounter, we will use the Closers Move and Close their own Conductor strategy.

Strat: Closers Move/Close Own

Starter Players will always ping from the Starting Plate on their side.

Closer Players will always go to the Plate that matches their partner's ping (it will change between rooms).

Closer Guardians will collect the resonance their conductor needs to close from other sides, instead of picking up the resonance that their plate generates and closing another Closer guardian's conductor.

When the encounter starts all plates and conductors are deactivated, a Boss Tormentor will spawn near all the Closing Plates, whoever gets the final blow on the Tormentor will receive the Stolen Valor buff needed to know which resonance type closes the Conductor. Therefore, both players in a team should deal with it together, but the guardian in charge of the Starting Plate must ensure that the Closer Guardian is the one to actually kill it.

Once the Tormentor is dead the plates and conductor on that side will activate. The Starter Guardian will go to the Starting Plate on their side and the Closer Guardian will go to the Closing Plate that matches that Starting Plate ping.

They will do the usual starting ping and three returning pings to spawn all resonance, but nobody picks it up yet. We will let the Plate Chain break to give the Closer Guardian time to go get the resonance they need to close their Conductor, doing this will spawn a Subjugator near the Closing Plate, but the Starter Guardian can easily go there and deal with it alone while their partner is away picking up the correct resonance.

Once the Closer Guardian has their resonance they will go back to their Conductor, the Starter Guardian should have killed the Subjugator already and returned to their Starting Plate to pick up the resonance that spawned near it. When both are ready they will do a last ping to close the Conductor.

When all three sides Conductors are closed the Resonant Chest will spawn and all Guardians should deposit their resonance to extend the timer. When everyone is resonance free an Unstoppable Champion alongside a few more ads will spawn near the exit door, killing it and all the ads will open the exit door.

Repeat the same process for all three rooms. The only difference between rooms is the size and the scrambled Plate pairing.

Room Three tips

  • All Tormentors spawn very closely together, everyone should focus on killing all of them and whoever gets Stolen Valor should make a general callout of the resonance needed to close the Conductors from Left to Right
  • Since it is the last room, spawning only one set of resonance is enough to close the Conductors, we don’t need extra resonance to extend the timer

Preparation/Short Intro

Fashion

Before starting the encounter each guardian will need to make sure they have a distinctive armor set and ghost. Shaders don't matter for the armor, but they do work as a distinguishing factor for the ghost.

After you have made yourself stand out with your armor and ghost it is time to take notes of the rest of your fireteam members, these notes will make the Saving the Guardians mechanic a lot easier so keep them somewhere easily accessible during the encounter.

The best way of formatting your notes would be:
[GUARDIAN NAME]: [GUARDIAN ARMOR DESCRIPTION] - [GUARDIAN GHOST DESCRIPTION]
Make sure the armor and ghost descriptions are descriptive enough, as you will need to quickly associate them with the guardian name when you see them in-game.

There are three different mechanics in this encounter, two of them, Inside and Outside, will take place in the Wall Zone, the other one, Saving the Guardians, will take place in the Spawn Zone.

As soon as the encounter starts three guardians will be isolated and teleported to a different version of the Wall Zone each, there is a glass wall that prevents them from going to the Spawn Zone. This is the Inside Team. The other three guardians will stay in the full encounter room, this is the Outside Team.

The Inside

If you are one of the teleported Guardians, you will be by yourself in a copy of the Wall Zone of the encounter room.

The room has three statues that represent you and the other two guardians teleported to their own version of the Wall Zone, these three statues will be holding a different 2D shape each (Triangle, Circle and Square). Guardian statues position and the shapes they hold are consistent across all three Inside rooms.

Your wall will be rotating two shapes: either two of the shape your statue is holding or one of the shape your guardian is holding and any of the other two.

This applies to every guardian teleported inside a Wall Zone room, making it a total of six shapes: two triangles, two circles and two squares, distributed between the three guardians' walls, two each.

Example: Wall Shapes Combinations

If your statue is holding a triangle your wall can show any of these combinations:

  • Triangle > Triangle
  • Triangle > Circle
  • Triangle > Square

Inside the room you will also find a few minor ads and two major hive knights. When you kill a hive knight, it will drop a shape as an interact to pick-up buff. This shape will always be one of the two shapes showing in you wall. The other knight would drop the other shape.

Example: Knight Shape Drops

If your wall is showing Triangle > Circle:

  • One knight will drop the Triangle Shape
  • The other knight will drop the Circle Shape

Shapes are limited, if a knight drops the Triangle, the other knight will 100% drop the Circle.

The main mechanic of this room is trading your shapes with the other Inside guardians.

When a knight drops a shape and you pick it up you can interact with the statues of your teleported partners, doing so will remove your buff, remove the shape you picked up and used on the statue from your wall, and it will give it to the guardian's wall whose statue you interacted with, making it available for them when they kill a knight.

3D Shape buffs

If you kill both knights and pick up the shape buffs they drop without dunking any first you will create a 3D shape buff.

Dunking a 3D shape buff in one of your teleported partners statues will get rid of the buff without any consequences for you or the guardian whose statue you interacted with.

This is useful if you have picked up the wrong shape buff and want to get rid of it without giving it to another guardian.

Ogres

A hive ogre will spawn in your room if both knights are dead and one of the shapes in your wall is unavailable to be picked up.

A shape is unavailable to be picked up if:

  • You killed the knight and didn't pick it up, making it despawn after some time
  • You picked up both shapes, forming a 3D and dunked them on a statue, they are still on your wall but the knights are dead

Killing the ogre will spawn as many knights as shapes you have in your wall, with a maximum of two knights.

  • If you have only one shape: only one knight will spawn, it will drop that shape if killed
  • If you have two shapes: two knights will spawn, dropping one of the shapes each.
  • If you have more than two shapes: First shapes in, First shapes out. Two knights will spawn, they will drop the shapes that were in your wall first. If you give those two shapes away another ogre will spawn, killing it will spawn new knights, those new knights will drop the next shapes.

Your main goal in this part of the encounter is to get out of the room though the glass wall that separates the Wall Zone from the Spawn Zone.

For you to be able to get out, three conditions must be met:

1. You are holding a 3D Shape made up of two symbols different than the symbol your statue is holding.

Example: Condition 1

If your statue is holding Triangle you can get out using:

  • Cylinder: Circle + Square
  • Sphere: Circle + Circle
  • Cube: Square + Square

2. You have traded the first two shapes you had on your wall when you were teleported to the room. It does not matter if one of those two shapes is given back to you, as long as you gave it away to another guardian first.

3. Your statue on the Outside is matching the 3D shape you are holding from Contition 1: this is the Outside Guardian's job, you don't have to worry about it.

While Condition 3 depends on the Outside players, you do need to communicate to them the 2D shapes the statues you see are holding from Left to Right as soon as the encounter starts, they need this information to do their job.

The easiest way to fulfill the two conditions that deppend on you (1 and 2) is using what's known as the Two of own, then distribute strat. The strat is divided in two steps:

Step 1: Two of Own

The goal of this first step is to make your wall rotate only the shape that your guardian's statue is holding.

To accomplish this, every guardian that has a shape on their wall different than the one their statue is holding must kill the knight that drops it, grab it, and give it to the statue of the guardian that is actually holding it.

If done correctly, everyone should have two of their own shape rotating on their wall, if you do, communicate it to your partners; everyone should finish this step before moving on to step 2.

Example 1: Two of Own

Your statue is holding Triangle, your wall is showing Triangle > Circle.

You kill a knight and it drops the Triangle. You want the Circle to drop so you ignore it and kill the other knight, which will drop the Circle.

You pick up the Circle, find the statue that is holding it, and interact with it, giving it to the Circle Guardian.

One of the other guardians would have a Triangle on their wall that they don't need; they would do the same and deposit their Triangle in your statue, giving it to you.

Now you have Triangle > Triangle on your wall. Step 1 is done; make sure to let the other guardians know.

Example 2: Two of Own

Your statue is holding Triangle, your wall is showing Triangle > Triangle.

You got lucky: Step 1 is already done for you when you got teleported in, you don't need to do anything other than letting your partners know you are ready for Step 2.

Step 2: Distribute

Once every guardian that got teleported to an inside room has two of the same shape on your wall (the one their statue is holding) you are ready for this step.

In this step, your goal is to give the other Inside Guardians your two shapes (one each).

  • Kill one of the knights so it drops one of the shapes and give it to the leftmost statue that is not yours.
  • Kill the other knight to drop the other shape and give it to the rightmost statue that is not yours.

Distributing from left to right makes it easier for you to remember which statue you have already distributed one of your shapes to and which one you haven't.

All you need to do now is wait for the other two guardians to do the same.

If everyone on the Inside does this correctly, you all will fulfill both Conditions 1 and 2:

  • You got rid of the two shapes that match your statue's shape, making sure your wall rotates the two shapes that don't.
  • By this point, you have also already traded away both the shapes you had on your wall initially.

Once your wall shows two shapes that are different from the one your statue is holding, kill the knights and pick up both shapes to create the 3D shape you need. If the Outside players have done their job correctly, you should be able to get out of the room through the glass.

Example: Distribute

Your statue is the one on the left and it is holding Triangle, your wall is showing Triangle > Triangle after doing Step 1 correctly.

You kill one of the knights, it drops one of the Triangles, you give it to the statue on the middle.

You kill the other knight and it drops the other Triangle, you give it to the statue on the right.

Now both of your teleported partners have a Triangle.

They will be doing the same with their shapes:
The guardian whose statue is holding a Circle will give one of their two Circles to you.
The guardian whose statue is holding a Square will give one of their two Squares to you.

Your wall will now have Circle > Square. Respawn the knights by killing the ogre and kill them to drop both of those shapes. Pick them up to make your 3D shape (Cylinder) and get out of the room; your job is done.

At some point during this part of the encounter, you will be interrupted by the Saving the Guardians mechanic. Once that part is over, you'll be back to your room to finish your job.

The Outside

You and two other guardians stay in the encounter room, you will need to go to the Wall Zone, where the three statues are, these statues match the guardians who got teleported inside and are holding 3D shapes.

3D Shapes

A 3D shape is a combination of two 2D shapes:

  • Pyramid: Triangle + Triangle
  • Sphere: Circle + Circle
  • Cube: Square + Square
  • Prism: Triangle + Square
  • Cone: Triangle + Circle
  • Cylinder: Circle + Square

As one of the outside guardians, one of your skills should be to identify which 2D shapes are part of a 3D shape.

Your goal is to match the outside statues' 3D Shapes with the 3D Shape the Inside guardians will be using to get out.

Since the guardians on the inside are using the Two of own, then distribute strat we know that the 3D shapes they will use to get out will be formed by the opposite shapes of what their statue is holding. We need the Inside Team to give us a simple Left to Right callout of their statues' shapes.

With that information we can do our job using the main mechanic of the Outside room: Dissection.

Outside Knights

Hive knights will spawn on the Outside just like they do on the Inside, the main difference is that the Outside spawns three knights instead of two, and the shapes buff they drop are fixed to the knight location.

  • The Left Knight will always drop Circle
  • The Mid Knight will always drop Triangle
  • The Right Knight will always drop Square

When all knights are dead and no shapes are available, two Unstoppable ogres will spawn: one on the left and one on the right. Killing them will make the knights respawn.

Dissection

Dissection is the Outside interaction with the statues and their shapes.

  • Dissecting a shape from a statue means you want to remove that 2D shape from the 3D shape the statue holds.
  • Dissection is a two statue process: the 2D shape that you dissect from a first statue will swap with the 2D shape you dissect from the second statue. When you dissect a shape from the first statue its pedestal will glow; that means the statue is waiting for you to dissect a 2D shape from another statue so they can swap.
  • Dissection is a one person job; more than one person dissecting makes it harder.
  • The guardian on the outside who wants to be in charge of the dissection should call it out; the other two will ad-clear for them.

You will need to swap 2D shapes between the statues using dissection to ensure Condition 3 is met for the Inside guardians.

Example: Dissection

  • Statue 1 is holding a Prism (Triangle + Square)
  • Statue 2 is holding a Sphere (Circle + Circle)
  • You pick up a Triangle and interact with Statue 1; its pedestal will glow.
  • You pick up a Circle and interact with Statue 2. The statues will swap their dissected 2D shapes, and Statue 1's pedestal glow will dim.
  • Statue 1 will now be holding a Cylinder (Circle + Square)
  • Statue 2 will now be holding a Cone (Triangle + Circle)

The Triangle you dissected from Statue 1 swapped with the Circle you dissected from Statue 2.

Example: Full Outside Cycle

The Inside Guardians give you their statue callout: TCS

On the outside the statues:

  • Left is holding a Cone (Triangle + Circle)
  • Mid is holding a Cylinder (Circle + Square)
  • Right is holding a Prism (Square + Triangle)

Let's focus on the Left Statue first; it is holding a Cone (Triangle + Circle) and we want it to be holding a Cylinder (Square + Circle) because that is the opposite of Triangle and is the 3D shape the Left statue guardian from the inside is going to use to get out.

Since a Cone is Triangle + Circle and we want a Cylinder, which is Square + Circle the first thing we need to do is dissect the Triangle that we don't want from the shape.

We kill the knight in the middle (because it is the one that drops the Triangle buff), pick up the buff, and interact with the Left statue; its pedestal will start to glow, meaning is waiting for you to dissect another shape to swap the Triangle with.

We want the Triangle to be replaced by a Square to create a Cylinder. We see that the statue on the Right is holding a Prism (Square + Triangle), and since the callout for that statue is S, that means that it does not need that Square. So, we kill the knight on the right (the one that drops the Square buff) and interact with the statue.

The dissection process is done; the Triangle we dissected from the statue on the Left will swap with the Square we dissected from the statue on the Right.

Now the statues look like this:

  • Left is holding a Cylinder (Square + Circle). Done
  • Mid is holding a Cylinder (Circle + Square)
  • Right is holding a Pyramid (Triangle + Triangle)

The Left callout was a T, and the statue is holding a Cylinder; we don't need to interact with that statue again, so let's focus on the other two.

The Mid statue has a Cylinder, and we want it to be a Prism. We need to dissect a Circle from it, so let's kill the last knight that is still alive (on the Left); it will drop the Circle buff for us.

We will interact with the Mid statue to dissect the Circle from it. Its pedestal will start to glow.

Since all the knights are dead, two unstoppable ogres will spawn. The Ad-Clearers should kill them as soon as possible; that will make new knights respawn.

The shape that needs to replace the Circle we just dissected is a Triangle. The Right statue has a Pyramid (two triangles), and only needs one so let's kill the mid knight, grab the triangle that it drops, and dissect it from the Right statue.

The shapes will swap, and the final state of each statue is:

  • Left is holding a Cylinder (Square + Circle). Done
  • Mid is holding a Prism (Triangle + Square). Done
  • Right is holding a Cone (Circle + Triangle). Done

Our job is done; all we need to do now is wait for the Inside Team to finish theirs, and they should be able to get out.

At some point during this part of the encounter, you will be interrupted by the Saving the Guardians mechanic. That should be the main focus until it is done.

Saving the Guardians

This mechanic takes place in the Spawn Zone, where the six statues are located.

At two different moments during the encounter, The Witness will kill a fraction of the fireteam, dropping their ghost in place.

Ghosts and Statues

  • The non-dead guardians will only see all their statues and the empty pedestals where the dead guardians' statues should be. Their job is to bring the ghost of a dead guardian to the empty pedestal where the statue of the ghost's owner is.
  • Each dead guardian will be able to see one statue in one of the empty (glowing for them) pedestals; this statue will never be their own. Their job is to comunicate to the alive guardians whose statue they see and on which pedestal it is.
  • Dunking a ghost in the wrong pedestal will kill the guardian holding the ghost

This is when the notes we took before the encounter started come in handy.

The alive guardians will use their ghost description notes to identify whose ghost they picked up.

The dead guardians will use their armor description notes to identify the guardian whose statue they see in the glowing pedestal.

Interruption: During the mechanics

While both Inside and Outside guardians are doing their job, The Witness will interrupt the mechanics and kill all three of the Inside guardians.

Their ghosts will spawn in the Outside room, available for the Outside guardians to pick up, one each.

The Outside guardians should quickly go through their notes before picking up a ghost to know whose ghost they are picking up, then go to the Spawn Zone and stand in front of the six statues, waiting for one of the dead guardians to give them the combination of Guardian Name and Pedestal Position callout for the guardian whose ghost they picked up. Dunk your ghost in the pedestal when they do and go back to your job in the Wall Zone.

The Inside guardian's cameras will stay in the Inside room. To be able to do the mechanics, they need to switch their death cam to spectate one of the alive guardians. When they grab a ghost and go to the Spawn Zone, they will be able to see three non-glowing statues (alive guardians) and three glowing pedestals, one of them with a statue. Take a look at your notes, identify the guardian, and give the alive guardians a Guardian Name and Pedestal Number callout. When the guardian who grabbed your ghost dunks it in the correct statue, you will be back in your Inside Room.

Interruption: After the mechanics

After all the Inside guardians leave their Inside rooms with their 3D shapes, the Imminent End timer will disappear, and a few minor ads alongside two Unstoppable ogres will spawn. Once all of them are dead, a new 1-minute Imminent End timer will appear, and 5 guardians will be calcified, leaving only 1 guardian alive to save them.

The mechanics are exactly the same for the dead guardians. You will see a statue that represents one of the other calcified guardians in one of the glowing pedestals; call it out by Guardian Name and Pedestal Position.

The alive guardian' job is easier this time; you don't need to go through your notes to make sure you are picking up the right ghost. The dead guardians' ghosts will be right next to their bodies, which still have the name tag above their heads. When you get a callout, look for the guardian's calcified body, pick up the ghost that is near it, and dunk it in the called-out pedestal. The dead guardian will be revived and can help you get the other ghosts to their pedestals.

Encounter Explanation

Resonance

The resonance buff is back in this encounter, but the way of obtaining it is different.
The Witness arms that we saw and ignored during the first three encounters will be our source of it.

To get a specific type of resonance we will need to get inside the attack of the arm we want the resonance from:

  • Triangle: Ceiling arm. Triangular attack that targets a guardian. Get inside the triangle before it explodes for a couple of seconds to get the resonance
  • Circle: Ground arm. Circular attack with a fixed spawn point. Get inside the circle for a couple of seconds before the bees kill you to get the resonance
  • Square: Wall arm. Horizontal beam parallel to the ground that targets a guardian. Get inside the beam area for a couple of seconds before the attack starts to get the resonance

There is a sound cue that starts when you enter the attack and ends when you obtain the resonance.

If we look at the Witness we can see six glyphs (buttons) on their chest, our goal is to destroy all six buttons to start a damage phase.
To destroy a button we will need the Glyphbreaker buff.

Glyphbreaker

We will obtain the Glyphbreaker buff as a reward for destroying one of the Witness arms. To destroy an arm we need to shoot at its wrist while being inside its attack and having a specific resonance-type buff.

To figure out which type of resonance an arm needs to break we will need to do a first wrist break while being briefly inside its attack (we don't want to be inside the attack for too long; if we are, we might accidently get its resonance type), that will destroy a surface layer of the wrist revealing one of the next three colors:

  • Red with square symbols: the arm needs Square resonance to be destroyed
  • Green with triangle symbols: the arm needs Triangle resonance to be destroyed
  • Yellow with elongated circle symbols: the arm needs Circle resonance to be destroyed

Once we know which resonance an arm needs to be broken we will go look for an arm that gives us that type of resonance, get inside its attack to get it and then come back to the initial arm to break it while being inside its attack (again, a quick in and out should be enough to break the arm; if we stay inside the attack for longer than needed, we will get its resonance and overwrite the one we went to get to break it).

When the arm breaks we will get the Glyphbreaker buff.

You can only break one Witness glyph with your Glyphbreaker buff, to break more glyphs you will need to reacquire it.

Cleanse

With the resonance buff coming back, its x3 stack restriction still applies, to cleanse your resonance stacks and avoid dying if you get a fourth you need to cleanse yourself from it.

To cleanse you need to be standing in the pool of light that is in the floating island in the center of the arena, when someone breaks a Witness glyph, the pool will light up, and everyone inside it will be cleansed of resonance.

When the encounter starts, all three different Witness arms will spawn and start doing their attacks, minor ads will also spawn.

The runners should start doing their job as soon as the encounter starts and the ad-clearers should be covering them while they do it from the endless Screebs, Scorn Chieftains and Weavers.

When a runner breaks a glyph, a Strand Subjugator will spawn on the left side, and a Stasis Subjugator will spawn on the right side. Breaking more glyphs will not spawn any additional Subjugators if both are still alive.

In every glyph breaking mechanic phase there will be a Witness test.

Witness Test

When one of the runners breaks an arm, there is a chance the arm glows orange and screams when broken. This means a Witness Test has been triggered.

The Witness will spawn a total of 14 Darkness Cruxes in front of them. To pass the test, the fireteam needs to shoot enough Cruxes so the ones that are still on create a shape that matches the broken arm resonance type (the type that the arm would give you if you stood in its attack, not the one that was used to break it).

After the test is done, The Witness will do an insta-kill ground attack; jump to avoid it.

When five glyphs have been destroyed and one runner has the Glyphbreaker buff to destroy the last one, they will call for Damage. Everyone will gather on the floating island, the last glyph will be broken, the floating island will rise to The Witness chest's height, and the damage phase starts.

Damage Phase

The Witness will do three attack cycles before ending the damage phase.
Witness attack cycle:

  • First Beam Attack: a horizontal attack similar to the Wall hand.
  • Second Beam Attack: the same as the first beam; its area of effect will never overlap with the first beam attack. Run to the area where the first attack happened, and you will always be safe.
  • Insta-Kill Ground Attack: the same as the end of the Test Ground Attack; jump to avoid it. This attack happens after or at the same time as the Second Beam Attack. It may not occur during the first attack cycle.

Once the damage phase ends, the island will go back to its original position, and six new glyphs will appear.

Final Stand

During final stand, you will have only a brief attack cycle (faster than the normal damage phases one) to damage the witness.

If you have not killed the Witness by the time the attack cycle ends, they will wipe the entire fireteam.
